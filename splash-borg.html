Splash-Borg

Gazo Editor's Note: As beings who highly value dry, organized environments and physical paper clipboards, we Gazos generally avoid massive aquatic zones. We requested a Gloober to document Splash-Borg. Because they only wanted to talk about the water, a Gembo construction foreman had to step in to document the actual infrastructure. Their unedited co-authored draft is below.
Splash-Borg
Location: The Great River Basin
Main Purpose: Relaxation and High-Velocity Sliding
Constructed By: Gembo Local Union 402
Managed By: Gloober Aquatics Council
"The water embraces you like a good friend. Also do not run on the wet concrete because you will fall."
— Welcome sign at the main gate

Overview

[Written by a Gloober]: Hello! Splash-Borg is our favorite place on the whole planet. It is a very big water park and nature preserve where the water is always perfectly cool and refreshing. We built it so every Glag can come here and wash away their stress. You can just float in the lazy river all day and we will bring you snacks and listen to you talk about your feelings. It is very supportive and nice.

[Written by a Gembo]: I'm just adding this so people know Splash-Borg didn't just magically appear out of good feelings. It took us Gembos three years to dig out the main river basin. We poured roughly forty thousand tons of reinforced waterproof concrete. It's the most structurally sound recreation facility on Gluborg. The foundation alone could survive a meteor strike.

The Mega-Slides and Hydro-Tubes

[Written by a Gloober]: The slides are so much fun. The water pushes you really fast through these big colorful tubes and then you do a giant splash at the bottom! We put glowing bioluminescent kelp in the tunnels so it looks like you are sliding through the stars. It makes everyone smile very big, especially the Gigglers.

[Written by a Gembo]: What my green friend here means is that the hydro-tubes are powered by Class-4 industrial water pumps that cycle two million gallons of water an hour. We had to build specialized steel brackets just to keep the tubes from shaking apart when a heavy Glaaber tries to go down them. We also had to install heavy-duty grates because the Gleebers keep sneaking in at night to skateboard in the dry overflow pipes.

Snacks and Recreation

[Written by a Gloober]: When you are done swimming you get very hungry. So we have a really nice snack bar. We sell wet kelp-dogs and fresh solar fruit juice! It is important to stay hydrated even when you are already inside the water.

[Written by a Gembo]: The snack bar is a load-bearing structure. We also sell high-protein rock-cakes for the construction crews on their lunch break. You can't work a twelve-hour shift on just kelp and fruit juice.

Trivia

  • Gloober: If a pink Cheerer falls in the wave pool, their confetti turns into a very colorful mush. We politely ask them to wear a shower cap.
  • Gembo: The filtration system has to be cleaned twice a week because of that exact confetti problem. It's a nightmare for the plumbing.
  • Gloober: Human visitors from Ambassador Vance's ships say Splash-Borg is the most relaxing place in the galaxy!
  • Gembo: We had to build extra-wide stairs for the Great Glag to visit once. He didn't even swim, he just drank half the wave pool and left.
