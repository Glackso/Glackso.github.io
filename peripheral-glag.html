Overview

Peripheral Glag is the central figure of Gluborgian mythology, widely worshipped by Gigglers, Cheerers, and generally accepted by the rest of the planet. According to planetary lore, Peripheral Glag is an all-seeing, omnipresent entity that wraps around the very edges of the universe, holding reality together.

However, as Gazos, we are bound by the principles of objective reality. To be completely candid—or to "keep it a buck," as the Gleebers say—there is absolutely zero empirical data to support the existence of this entity. Gazos are not known for faith; we are known for spreadsheets. And currently, Peripheral Glag's spreadsheet is entirely blank.

The Track Record (Or Lack Thereof)

The primary claim regarding Peripheral Glag is that it is the direct parent of The Great Glag, placing its existence somewhere before the Proto-Gazoid era. Let us look at the raw data: if we assume this is true, Peripheral Glag has done absolutely nothing since that moment.

During the Great Split, Peripheral Glag did not intervene. When the Kepler-Gluborg Accords were signed, it did not provide legal counsel. When Splash-Borg’s main filtration pump broke in 2089, did Peripheral Glag fix it? No. Gembo Local Union 402 fixed it. From a purely historical perspective, if Peripheral Glag is out there, it is practicing a level of non-interference that borders on extreme apathy.

The Silent Investigation

We Gazos remember the DNA Controversy. We know exactly what happens when we publicly question the mythology of The Great Glag or Peripheral Glag: the other species get very, very mad. We have no desire to clean up another retaliatory confetti strike from the Cheerers, nor do we want the Gigglers to cry.

Therefore, the Gazo Archives Division has initiated a covert, long-term protocol. We are quietly cross-referencing every single historical document, geological survey, and astronomical reading in total silence. Until we find definitive proof—or conclusively debunk the myth—we will continue to politely nod when a Giggler blesses us in the name of the Peripheral one.
