Gazos

Gazos
Coloration: Blue
Average Size: 1 meter (diameter)
Primary Roles: Inventors, Industrialists, Archivists
Temperament: Highly Neutral / Logical
"Innovation through stabilization; a place for every Glag, and every Glag in its place."
— Common Gazo saying

Overview

Gazos are a blue-hued species of Glag native to the rich, temperate, and highly stable regions of Gluborg. Averaging about 1 meter in diameter, Gazos are the primary industrialists, inventors, and record-keepers of our planet. We are also the proud founders and maintainers of Gazowiki. Despite our control over planetary industries, we view ourselves strictly as facilitators rather than leaders. A functioning machine requires all its gears, and we are simply the gear that keeps the records.

Society and Industry

Gazo settlements are highly organized, usually built in resource-rich environments safe from extreme weather. We dedicate our lives to the pursuit of stability and efficiency. When the Gembos need better agricultural tools, we design them. When the Gleebers developed an obsession with skateboards, we repurposed our factories to mass-produce polyurethane wheels for them.

Because we manage the logistics of Gluborg's economy, some human traders mistakenly assume we are the "ruling class" of the planet. We strongly reject this notion. We are entirely equal to the Glaabers, Gloobers, and all other species. Our wealth is simply a byproduct of our organizational habits, and it is routinely redistributed to fund planetary infrastructure.

Planetary Neutrality

If there is a defining Gazo trait, it is our unwavering neutrality. We do not engage in planetary disputes, nor do we typically represent Gluborg in intergalactic matters (we happily leave diplomacy to the future-seeking Gigglers). Our role is strictly objective. We document history as it happens, ensuring that the achievements of all Glags are preserved without bias.

Trivia

  • Gazos were the first to decode the human "internet" archives brought over by Ambassador Elias Vance's trade ships, which inspired the creation of this wiki.
  • Our blue coloration can slightly shift in saturation depending on how organized our immediate surroundings are. (A messy room leads to a duller blue).
  • We are historically terrible at dealing with the Cheerers. Their chaotic nature and constant deployment of confetti directly conflicts with our desire to keep our industrial floors swept.
  • Despite our advanced technology, we still prefer to use physical clipboards for daily tasks.
