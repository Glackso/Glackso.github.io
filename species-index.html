The spherical lifeforms of Gluborg are universally recognized for their highly specialized environmental adaptations. While all modern inhabitants fall under the general classification of Glaggles, planetary biologists and Gazo archivists recognize seven core species. Each species occupies a distinct ecological and societal niche, working in tandem to ensure the planet thrives.

Evolutionary History: The Great Split

According to the earliest geological and biological records, the modern Glag did not always exist in seven distinct forms. Originally, the planet was populated entirely by a single, unified ancestral species known as the Proto-gazoids.

As the population of Proto-gazoids expanded across the diverse biomes of Gluborg, they underwent a rapid and monumental evolutionary divergence known historically as The Great Split. To maximize resource efficiency and harmonize with the planet's varied environments, the Proto-gazoids naturally specialized into the seven core species we see today.

The Seven Core Species

Following The Great Split, the following seven species established themselves across Gluborg's distinct geographical zones:

  • Gazo: The blue-hued, highly stable archivist class. Gazos naturally gravitate toward the most resource-rich and environmentally stable regions of Gluborg, where they maintain the planetary archives and facilitate diplomatic relations.
  • Gembo: The agricultural backbone of the planet. Gembos are natural ranchers and farmers who inhabit the sprawling plains and fertile valleys, ensuring a steady food supply for all other species.
  • Glaggle (Standard / Giggler): The urbanites of Gluborg. These highly social spheres thrive in densely populated areas, forming the cultural and industrial core of the planet's massive metropolitan centers.
  • Gleeber: The mediators of space. Gleebers live in the crucial "in-between" zones, establishing comfortable suburban rings that bridge the chaotic energy of the Glaggle cities and the quiet, vast expanses of the Gembo farmlands.
  • Gloober: The aquatic specialists. Gloobers possess a deep biological affinity for water and are exclusively found residing near oceans, expansive lakes, and major river systems.
  • Glaaber: The pioneers. Exceptionally hardy, Glaabers choose to live on the rugged, untamed frontiers of Gluborg. They push the boundaries of known territories and thrive in harsh conditions.
  • Cheerer: The kinetic nomads. Cheerers thrive on movement and high activity, almost always found inhabiting areas of extreme transit—such as bustling downtown districts, migratory hubs, and interstellar spaceports.
