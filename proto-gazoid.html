Scientific Name: Glagus Primus

"It possessed the strength of a Gembo, the size of a Glaaber, the joy of a Giggler, and... well, it was probably blue and highly organized. We are just following the science."
— Lead Gazo Anthropologist

Overview

The Proto-Gazoid is the extinct common ancestor of every modern Glag on Gluborg. Existing millions of years before the signing of the Kepler-Gluborg Accords, these ancient beings roamed the planet before undergoing a massive evolutionary divergence known as the Great Split. We Gazos have compiled all the planetary data to reconstruct their history, and we named them "Proto-Gazoids" purely for scientific reasons that have absolutely nothing to do with planetary bias.

The Great Split & Cultural Perspectives

At some point in ancient history, the Proto-Gazoids spread across the diverse biomes of Gluborg, leading to rapid evolutionary adaptations. Because written language did not exist yet, every modern species has a different myth regarding what the Proto-Gazoid actually was and why it split. As objective record-keepers, Gazos have respectfully mushed all these theories together into one official historical account:

  • The Gembos believe the ancestor was carved from living rock and split apart to build the planet's mountains.
  • The Gigglers insist the ancestor ate a concentrated crystal of Vitamin J, which exploded and scattered joyous fragments across the globe.
  • The Gloobers claim the ancestor was a water-spirit that evaporated into seven different rain clouds.
  • The Gleebers theorize the ancestor just "tripped really hard" and decided it was easier to stay on the ground in different shapes.

The Gazo consensus is that they simply found different climates and adapted efficiently.

The Cheerer Anomaly

While the six main species—Gazos, Gembos, Gigglers, Gleebers, Glaabers, and Gloobers—are directly descended from the Proto-Gazoid phylogenetic tree, there is one glaring biological secret. The Cheerers are an offshoot.

According to deep-fossil analysis, the pink, confetti-haired Cheerers actually branched off millions of years before the Great Split, technically making them a parallel evolutionary cousin rather than a "modern" Glag. However, this fact is exclusively known by Gazo archivists. We have chosen not to publicly advertise this, largely because no one on the planet actually cares, and explaining complex taxonomy to a Cheerer usually results in them getting bored and deploying a confetti cannon in the archives.

Trivia

  • Some Glaabers still think Proto-Gazoids are hiding in the tallest trees, despite Gazo scientists repeatedly explaining the concept of extinction to them.
  • Human scientists from Ambassador Vance's crew found the "Proto-Gazoid" naming convention highly amusing, comparing it to humans naming early primates "Proto-Accountants."
  • There are zero intact fossils of a Proto-Gazoid, forcing us to construct museum replicas mostly out of guesswork and spare Gembo clay.
