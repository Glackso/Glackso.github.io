Internet Nostalgia

From RetroWiki, the free encyclopedia

Internet nostalgia refers to a cultural phenomenon where users reminisce about the early days of the World Wide Web, particularly the late 1990s and early 2000s. During this era, websites were largely static, text-heavy, and built using basic HTML tables and inline styling.

Design Trends

Common design trends included left-aligned sidebars, "web safe" fonts like Arial and Times New Roman, and default link colors. When a user clicked a link, it changed from its standard blue (#0000ee) to purple (#551a8b) to indicate it had been visited.

Because dynamic content management systems were not widely adopted by casual users, maintaining a site required manual coding or the use of early WYSIWYG editors.

Web Safe Colors

Due to the limitations of early monitors, designers often restricted themselves to a palette of 216 "web safe" colors to ensure their sites looked the same across different operating systems and browsers.
