Gluborg

Gluborg
Human Designation: Kepler-774b
Inhabitants: Glags (7 sub-species)
Status: Protected (Treaty of 2084)
Major Exports: Space-moss, Gazo-tech

Gluborg (known in the human star-charts as Kepler-774b) is a lush, temperate planet and the homeworld of the Glags. This wiki, Gazowiki, is maintained by the Gazos to document our shared history, culture, and day-to-day lives across the planet.

Species of Gluborg

The term "Glag" is an umbrella classification for the seven distinct sentient species that cohabit Gluborg. While we look and act differently, we share a common ancestor.

  • Gazos: Blue glags who live in rich, stable environments. We control industries, create inventions, and act as a neutral party in planetary affairs. As the creators of this wiki, we like to keep things organized.
  • Gembos: Red glags known for their hard work. They are the ranchers, agriculturists, and construction workers of Gluborg. They have a deep love for nature and are our best manufacturers.
  • Gigglers: Yellow, highly active beings full of "Vitamin J," which makes them incredibly joyous. They possess the unique ability to seek a better future and often act as our diplomats, though their main flaw is sometimes lacking direction.
  • Gleebers: Purple glags who are naturally laid-back and prefer living in suburban areas. They have become completely obsessed with skateboards ever since the humans showcased them.
  • Glaabers: Huge, orange glags measuring about 5 meters in diameter (five times the size of a normal glag). They are currently the least advanced species, possessing the lowest literacy rates, and are unfortunately easily manipulatable.
  • Gloobers: Green, highly supportive glags who have a deep, natural attachment to water and the environment.
  • Cheerers: Pink glags sporting an evolutionary mutation they absolutely love: confetti hair! They are biological jesters who tend to cause chaos. Though they have the lowest population overall, they always live in densely populated areas where many glags migrate.

The Galactic Treaty

Following the vast expansion of the Human Empire in the late 21st century, humans discovered Gluborg. Initial tensions were quickly resolved thanks to the foresight of our greatest diplomat.

John J. Gig, a prominent Giggler whose future-seeking abilities saw the potential for a golden age, stepped forward to negotiate. He met with human representative Ambassador Elias Vance.

Together, they signed the Kepler-Gluborg Accords. The treaty dictates that:

  1. Gluborg remains an independently governed planet, protected from human colonization.
  2. A permanent, peaceful trade route is established between Human-controlled space and Gluborg.
  3. No human may alter the atmospheric composition of Gluborg.

Thanks to John J. Gig and Ambassador Vance, our planet thrives, and human cargo ships are now a regular, welcomed sight in our skies.
